Plant Kingdom

Classification and features of major groups of plants (excluding angiosperms).

Introduction

The plant kingdom includes all photosynthetic organisms (and some that have lost photosynthetic ability). This chapter explores how plants are classified into various major groups and the key features of each.

Major Plant Groups

Plants are classified into groups: Algae, Bryophyta, Pteridophyta, Gymnospermae, and (in older editions) Angiosperms. But in the revised NCERT, the focus is on the first four.

Group Key Features Examples
Algae Simple, aquatic, photosynthetic organisms Chlamydomonas, Spirogyra
Bryophytes Non-vascular, lack true roots, stems, and leaves Mosses, Liverworts
Pteridophytes Vascular, reproduce via spores, no seeds Ferns, Selaginella
Gymnosperms Seed-producing, "naked seeds" Conifers, Cycads

Algae

Algae are simple, mostly aquatic, photosynthetic organisms. They may be unicellular or multicellular, and show varying pigments (like chlorophyll).

Type Characteristics Examples
Green Algae Contain chlorophyll a and b Chlamydomonas, Spirogyra
Brown Algae Contain chlorophyll c and fucoxanthin Laminaria, Fucus
Red Algae Contain phycoerythrin Polysiphonia, Gelidium

Bryophytes

Bryophytes (mosses, liverworts) are non-vascular plants. They lack true roots, stems, and leaves.

Type Characteristics Examples
Mosses Form dense green clumps Funaria, Polytrichum
Liverworts Flattened, thalloid structure Marchantia, Riccia

Pteridophytes

Pteridophytes are vascular plants that reproduce via spores (e.g. ferns). They have true roots, stems, and leaves but don‚Äôt produce seeds.

Feature Description
Vascular Tissues Have xylem and phloem
Reproduction Via spores, no seeds

Gymnosperms

Gymnosperms are seed-producing plants where seeds are not enclosed in an ovary (‚Äúnaked seeds‚Äù), e.g. conifers, cycads.

Feature Description

Summary

The plant kingdom exhibits huge diversity, from simple algae to more complex gymnosperms. Classification into major groups helps in understanding evolutionary patterns and organization of plant life.
