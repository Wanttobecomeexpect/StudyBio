Neural Control and Coordination

How neurons transmit signals, how nervous system organizes behaviour and reflexes ‚Äî basic neural physiology.

Introduction to Nervous System

Nervous system enables rapid coordination through electrical signals. Divided into central nervous system (CNS) and peripheral nervous system (PNS) including autonomic division.

Component Function
CNS Processes information and coordinates responses.
PNS Transmits signals between CNS and the rest of the body.

Neuron Structure & Conduction

Neuron types: sensory, motor, interneurons. Action potential: depolarisation (Na‚Å∫ influx), repolarisation (K‚Å∫ efflux), refractory period. Myelination accelerates conduction (saltatory conduction).

Neuron Type Function
Sensory Neuron Transmits sensory information to CNS.
Motor Neuron Conveys signals from CNS to muscles or glands.
Interneuron Connects sensory and motor neurons within CNS.

Synapse & Neurotransmitters

Synapse transmits signals chemically (neurotransmitters) or electrically. Common neurotransmitters: acetylcholine, noradrenaline, dopamine, GABA. Synaptic transmission involves vesicle release, receptor binding and termination.

Neurotransmitter Function
Acetylcholine Involved in muscle activation and memory.
Dopamine Regulates mood, reward, and motor control.
GABA Acts as an inhibitory neurotransmitter.

CNS & PNS ‚Äî Organization

Brain regions (forebrain, midbrain, hindbrain) and spinal cord organisation; peripheral nerves and cranial nerves; autonomic nervous system: sympathetic (fight/flight) and parasympathetic (rest/digest).

Division Key Features
Sympathetic Prepares body for stress (increases heart rate, dilates pupils).
Parasympathetic Promotes relaxation (slows heart rate, stimulates digestion).

Reflex Action & Reflex Arc

Reflexes are rapid, involuntary responses mediated by reflex arc: receptor ‚Üí sensory neuron ‚Üí (interneuron) ‚Üí motor neuron ‚Üí effector. Examples: knee-jerk reflex.

Component Role
Receptor Detects stimulus.
Sensory Neuron Transmits signal to CNS.
Motor Neuron Conveys response to effector.

Sense Organs (Overview)

Sense organs (eye, ear, nose, tongue, skin) transduce external stimuli into neural signals. Basic idea of photoreceptors, mechanoreceptors and chemoreceptors is covered.

Sense Organ Stimulus Detected
Eye Light (photoreceptors).
Ear Sound and balance (mechanoreceptors).
Skin Touch, pressure, temperature (mechanoreceptors).

Summary

Neural control provides fast, targeted responses. Understanding neuron function, synaptic transmission and higher neural organization underpins behavior, learning and reflexes.

Topic Key Takeaway
Neurons Transmit electrical signals for rapid communication.
Synapse Facilitates signal transfer between neurons.
Reflex Arc Enables quick, involuntary responses to stimuli.
