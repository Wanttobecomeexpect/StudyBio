Animal Kingdom

Classification of animals based on structural organisation, body plan and evolutionary relationships.

Introduction

The animal kingdom includes multicellular, heterotrophic organisms. Their classification is based on body organization, symmetry, coelom, and other morphological traits. :contentReference[oaicite:23]{index=23}

Basis of Classification

Animals are grouped based on symmetry (radial or bilateral), number of germ layers (diploblastic or triploblastic), presence of coelom, segmentation, and other anatomical features. :contentReference[oaicite:24]{index=24}

Non-Chordates (Invertebrates)

These are animals without a notochord. Example phyla: Porifera (sponges), Coelenterata/Cnidaria, Platyhelminthes (flatworms), Nematoda, Annelida, Arthropoda, Mollusca, Echinodermata. :contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}

Chordates

Chordates have a notochord, dorsal hollow nerve cord, pharyngeal slits, post-anal tail at some life stage. They include: fishes (Chondrichthyes, Osteichthyes), amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals. :contentReference[oaicite:26]{index=26}

Summary

Animal Kingdom classification helps us understand the vast variety of animals, their evolutionary relationships, and structural complexity. It provides a foundation for studying physiology, ecology, and behaviour.
