Morphology of Frog (Typical Vertebrate)

The body of frog includes head and trunk, no neck or tail. It has limbs (forelimbs and hind limbs), skin (with mucous glands), and adaptations for both aquatic and terrestrial life. Eyes, nostrils, and tympanum are present. Hind limbs are muscular for jumping/swimming; forelimbs support landing. :contentReference[oaicite:39]{index=39}

  • Circulatory system: Heart and blood vessels distribute nutrients, oxygen. (While detailed human physiology comes later, basic circulation in frog is covered.) :contentReference[oaicite:41]{index=41}
  • Respiratory system: Lungs + skin ‚Äî frog can respire through skin (cutaneous respiration) and lungs. Helps in aquatic and terrestrial respiration. :contentReference[oaicite:42]{index=42}

Summary

Structural organisation in animals from cells to organ systems enables diverse functions and adaptability. Studying a model vertebrate (frog) gives insight into anatomy and physiology. Basic understanding of tissues and organ systems lays foundation for higher physiology and biology.
