Anatomy of Flowering Plants

Internal structure of flowering plants ‚Äî tissues, tissue-systems and organ anatomy.

Introduction ‚Äî What is Anatomy?

Anatomy studies internal structure of plants ‚Äî how cells are organized into tissues, and tissues into organs. It helps understand function, adaptation and classification.

Aspect Description
Focus Internal structure of plants
Importance Understanding function, adaptation, and classification

Plant Tissues

Tissues are groups of similar cells performing common functions. Broadly divided into:

  • Meristematic tissues ‚Äî cells actively dividing (apical, intercalary, lateral meristems)
  • Permanent tissues ‚Äî simple (parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma) and complex (xylem and phloem)
Type Characteristics Examples
Meristematic Actively dividing cells Apical meristem
Permanent Specialized for specific functions Parenchyma, Xylem

Tissue Systems

There are three main tissue systems in higher plants:

  • Epidermal tissue system ‚Äî outer protective layer (epidermis, cuticle, stomata, sometimes hairs/trichomes)
  • Ground tissue system ‚Äî fills most of plant interior; functions in storage, support, photosynthesis
  • Vascular tissue system ‚Äî conducting system: xylem (water & mineral transport) + phloem (food transport)
Tissue System Function Components
Epidermal Protection Epidermis, Cuticle
Ground Storage, Support Cortex, Pith
Vascular Conduction Xylem, Phloem

Anatomy of Dicotyledonous vs Monocotyledonous Plants

Dicots and monocots differ in tissue/organ-level structure. For roots, stems and leaves ‚Äî vascular bundle arrangement, presence/absence of cambium, pith, cortex, etc vary.

Feature Dicot Monocot
Vascular Bundles Arranged in a ring Scattered
Secondary Growth Present Absent
Leaf Anatomy Dorsiventral Isobilateral

Secondary Growth (in Woody Plants ‚Äî Dicots)

Secondary growth is due to lateral meristems (vascular cambium & cork cambium), leading to increase in girth (thickness) of roots and stems. Secondary xylem = wood. Secondary tissues provide mechanical support and conduction in woody plants.

Aspect Description
Vascular Cambium Produces secondary xylem and phloem
Cork Cambium Produces protective outer layer (bark)

Summary

Anatomy reveals how internal organization ‚Äî tissues, tissue-systems and organ-level structure ‚Äî supports functions like conduction, support, storage and growth. Differences between dicots and monocots underlie their structural adaptations. Understanding anatomy complements morphology and helps in classification, physiology, and ecology.
