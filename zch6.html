Introduction & Excretory Substances

Excretory products include CO‚ÇÇ, water, nitrogenous wastes (urea, uric acid, ammonia). Excretion prevents accumulation of toxic metabolites and maintains homeostasis.

Kidney ‚Äî Structure & Function

Kidneys consist of nephrons (functional units) ‚Äî renal corpuscle (glomerulus + Bowman‚Äôs capsule) and tubular system (proximal tubule, loop of Henle, distal tubule, collecting duct). Kidneys filter blood and form urine.

Urine Formation

Three main processes: glomerular filtration (non-selective), tubular reabsorption (useful substances returned to blood), tubular secretion (selective removal). Concentration of urine involves counter-current mechanism in loop of Henle.

Osmoregulation & ADH Role

Body maintains water/electrolyte balance via kidneys under hormonal control: ADH (vasopressin) increases water reabsorption; aldosterone affects sodium reabsorption. Osmoreceptors and thirst centre regulate fluid balance.

Summary
