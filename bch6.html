Cell ‚Äì The Unit of Life

Understanding the cell, its structure, organelles and significance as the fundamental unit of life.

Cell Theory

All living organisms are composed of cells; cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life. Functions of life (nutrition, respiration, growth, reproduction) take place at cellular level. Modern cell biology builds on this principle. (As per syllabus.)

Scientist Contribution
Schleiden Proposed that plants are made of cells.
Schwann Proposed that animals are made of cells.
Virchow Stated that cells arise from pre-existing cells.

Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

Prokaryotes lack a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles; eukaryotes have a defined nucleus, organelles and compartmentalisation. This division forms the basis of major life groups. Fundamental differences matter for structure, function & classification.

Feature Prokaryotic Cells Eukaryotic Cells
Nucleus Absent Present
Organelles Non-membrane bound Membrane-bound
Size Small (1-10 ¬µm) Larger (10-100 ¬µm)

Plant Cell vs Animal Cell

Both are eukaryotic but differ in some organelles and structures. Plant cells have cell wall, plastids (chloroplasts), large central vacuole; animal cells have flexible cell membrane, centrioles (in many), and different organelle arrangements ‚Äî reflecting difference in function (photosynthesis, rigidity vs mobility).

Feature Plant Cell Animal Cell
Cell Wall Present Absent
Chloroplasts Present Absent
Vacuole Large central vacuole Small or absent

Cell Organelles and Functions

Organelles include: nucleus (genetic control), endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, mitochondria (powerhouse), ribosomes (protein synthesis), plastids (in plants), lysosomes, vacuoles, microbodies ‚Äî each with specific roles. These organelles allow compartmentalisation and efficiency in cellular processes.

Organelle Function
Nucleus Controls cell activities and stores genetic material.
Mitochondria Produces energy (ATP) through respiration.
Ribosomes Synthesizes proteins.

Cell Envelope & Endomembrane System

Cell envelope: cell membrane (and in plants, cell wall) ‚Äî defines boundary, regulates exchange. Endomembrane system (ER, Golgi, vesicles, vacuoles) helps in transport, synthesis and processing of molecules within cell. Important for secretion, storage, and compartmental functions.

Component Function
Endoplasmic Reticulum Synthesis and transport of proteins and lipids.
Golgi Apparatus Processing and packaging of molecules.
Vesicles Transport of substances within the cell.

Cytoskeleton, Cilia/Flagella, Centrioles

Cytoskeleton ‚Äî network of microtubules, microfilaments provides shape, support and enables movement within cell. Cilia and flagella (in some cells) enable locomotion or movement of substances. Centrioles (in animal cells) help in cell division. These cellular structures enable dynamic behaviour and cell functions.

Structure Function
Cytoskeleton Provides shape and support to the cell.
Cilia/Flagella Enables movement of the cell or substances.
Centrioles Assist in cell division.

Summary

The cell is the fundamental unit of life; understanding its structure and components is essential to study biology. Differences among cell types reflect adaptation to different life forms (plants, animals, prokaryotes). Knowledge of organelles, membranes and cytoskeleton provides basis for physiology, genetics and molecular biology.

Key Concept Description
Cell Theory All living organisms are composed of cells.
Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Differences in structure and complexity.
Organelles Specialized structures for specific functions.
