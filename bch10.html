Plant Growth and Development

How plants grow, differentiate and respond to environmental cues ‚Äî role of hormones and external factors.

Introduction & Phases of Growth

Growth is defined as an irreversible increase in size and dry mass of an organism. In plants, growth occurs in three distinct phases:

  • Cell Division: The process of mitosis in meristematic tissues.
  • Cell Enlargement: Increase in cell size due to water uptake and vacuole expansion.
  • Cell Differentiation: Specialization of cells to form specific tissues and organs.
Phase Description Example
Cell Division Active mitosis in meristematic regions. Root and shoot apices.
Cell Enlargement Increase in cell size due to water absorption. Elongation of internodes.
Cell Differentiation Formation of specialized cells and tissues. Xylem and phloem formation.

Measurement of Growth

Growth in plants can be measured using various parameters such as length, area, volume, or dry weight. Growth patterns are described using relative and absolute growth rates, and growth curves.

Parameter Measurement Method Example
Length Measure elongation of roots or shoots. Root tip growth.
Area Measure leaf area using graph paper or software. Leaf expansion.
Dry Weight Weigh plant after drying in an oven. Biomass accumulation.

Plant Hormones (Phytohormones)

Plant hormones are chemical messengers that regulate growth and development. The major phytohormones include:

Hormone Function Example
Auxins Promote cell elongation and apical dominance. Indole Acetic Acid (IAA).
Gibberellins Stimulate stem elongation and seed germination. GA3 (Gibberellic Acid).
Cytokinins Promote cell division and delay senescence. Zeatin.
Abscisic Acid (ABA) Induces dormancy and stress responses. Leaf abscission.
Ethylene Promotes fruit ripening and leaf senescence. Banana ripening.

Photoperiodism & Vernalisation

Plants respond to the duration of light and dark periods (photoperiodism) and cold treatment (vernalisation) to regulate flowering and other processes.

Process Description Example
Photoperiodism Response to day/night length to control flowering. Short-day plants (rice), long-day plants (wheat).
Vernalisation Cold treatment to induce flowering. Winter wheat.

Seed Germination & Differentiation

Seed germination involves the activation of metabolic processes leading to radicle emergence. Differentiation refers to the formation of specialized cells and organs.

Process Factors Example
Germination Water, oxygen, temperature, hormones. Bean seed germination.
Differentiation Genetic and environmental cues. Root and shoot formation.

Summary

Plant growth and development are complex processes regulated by hormones, environmental factors, and genetic control. Understanding these processes is essential for advancements in agriculture, horticulture, and plant breeding.

Aspect Key Points
Growth Irreversible increase in size and mass.
Development Formation of organs and tissues.
Applications Agriculture, horticulture, plant breeding.
