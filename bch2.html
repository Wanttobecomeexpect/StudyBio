Biological Classification

Organizing living organisms into groups based on similarities and differences.

Introduction

Classification helps us understand relationships between different organisms. Early systems (like two-kingdom) were based on simple observations, but modern classification is more comprehensive.

Classification System Key Features
Two-Kingdom Plants and Animals
Five-Kingdom Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia

Five-Kingdom Classification

The current system classifies life into five kingdoms: Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia. Each kingdom is differentiated by cell type, nutrition, complexity, and reproduction.

Kingdom Cell Type Nutrition
Monera Prokaryotic Autotrophic/Heterotrophic
Protista Eukaryotic Autotrophic/Heterotrophic
Fungi Eukaryotic Saprophytic
Plantae Eukaryotic Autotrophic
Animalia Eukaryotic Heterotrophic

Kingdom Monera

Monera includes prokaryotic organisms like bacteria. They lack a membrane-bound nucleus. Some are autotrophic (cyanobacteria), others heterotrophic.

Reproduction is mostly asexual (binary fission), but some bacteria show conjugation.

Feature Description
Cell Structure Prokaryotic, no nucleus
Examples Bacteria, Cyanobacteria

Kingdom Protista

Protists are eukaryotic, mostly unicellular (some colonial). Includes algae, protozoa, and slime molds.

They show variety in nutrition: autotrophic protists (many algae) and heterotrophic (protozoans). Also reproduction may be asexual or sexual.

Group Example
Algae Chlamydomonas
Protozoa Amoeba

Kingdom Fungi

Fungi are eukaryotic, mostly multicellular, non-photosynthetic organisms. They absorb nutrition from surroundings (saprophytic).

They reproduce by spores; some have complex life cycles. Examples: Ascomycetes, Basidiomycetes.

Type Example
Ascomycetes Yeast
Basidiomycetes Mushroom

Viruses, Lichens, Viroids

Viruses are acellular, parasitic particles. Viroids are infectious RNA molecules. Lichens are symbiotic associations between fungi and algae or cyanobacteria.

Organism Key Feature
Virus Acellular, requires host
Viroid Infectious RNA
Lichen Fungi + Algae symbiosis

Summary

Classification into kingdoms helps us understand the huge diversity of life. Based on cellular structure, nutrition, reproduction, and organization, we organize organisms into five kingdoms and even special groups like viruses and lichens.

Kingdom Example
Monera Bacteria
Protista Amoeba
Fungi Mushroom
Plantae Mango Tree
Animalia Human
