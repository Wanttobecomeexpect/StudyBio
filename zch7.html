Locomotion and Movement

Bones, joints and muscles enable movement ‚Äî mechanical and biochemical basis of locomotion in animals.

Introduction & Types of Movement

Movement may be at cellular level (ciliary, amoeboid), or whole organism (locomotion). Musculoskeletal system provides forces and levers for movement.

Type of Movement Example
Amoeboid White blood cells
Ciliary Respiratory tract
Muscular Walking, running

Skeletal System ‚Äî Bones & Joints

Skeleton types: endoskeleton (vertebrates), exoskeleton (arthropods). Bone structure: compact & spongy bone, marrow. Joints: fibrous, cartilaginous, synovial (allow various movements).

Joint Type Movement Example
Fibrous Immovable Skull sutures
Cartilaginous Partially movable Intervertebral discs
Synovial Freely movable Knee, elbow

Muscular System & Muscle Contraction

Muscles (skeletal, smooth, cardiac). Skeletal muscle contraction via sliding filament mechanism ‚Äî actin & myosin crossbridge cycling powered by ATP. Role of Ca¬≤‚Å∫ and troponin/tropomyosin in regulation.

Muscle Type Characteristics Example
Skeletal Voluntary, striated Biceps, triceps
Cardiac Involuntary, striated Heart
Smooth Involuntary, non-striated Intestines

Human Locomotion (Overview)

Integration of bones, joints, muscles and neural control enables posture and movement (walking, running). Antagonistic muscle pairs (flexor/extensor) and motor unit concept are central to movement.

Component Function
Bones Provide structure and support
Joints Allow movement
Muscles Generate force for movement
Nerves Coordinate and control movement

Summary

Locomotion arises from mechanical architecture and biochemical energy conversion in muscles. Understanding structure & function of bones, joints and muscles is essential for physiology and biomechanics.

Aspect Key Points
Bones Provide support and protection
Joints Enable flexibility and movement
Muscles Convert biochemical energy to mechanical work
