Breathing and Exchange of Gases

Understanding how humans breathe, exchange gases, and transport oxygen and carbon dioxide throughout the body.

Introduction to Breathing

Breathing (external respiration) is the process of taking in oxygen-rich air and releasing carbon dioxide. It is a physical process, whereas respiration is a biochemical process involving oxidation of food to release energy.

Human Respiratory System

The human respiratory system includes nostrils ‚Üí nasal cavity ‚Üí pharynx ‚Üí larynx ‚Üí trachea ‚Üí bronchi ‚Üí bronchioles ‚Üí alveoli. Alveoli are thin-walled vascular sacs where gaseous exchange occurs.

Mechanism of Breathing

Breathing involves two steps:

  • Inspiration: Diaphragm contracts and flattens, rib cage expands ‚Äî thoracic volume increases, pressure decreases ‚Üí air enters lungs.
  • Expiration: Diaphragm relaxes, ribs move inward ‚Äî thoracic volume decreases, pressure increases ‚Üí air is expelled.

Exchange of Gases

Gas exchange occurs by diffusion:

  • O2 diffuses from alveoli ‚Üí blood (high to low concentration).
  • CO2 diffuses from blood ‚Üí alveoli.

Factors affecting diffusion: pressure gradients, solubility of gases, membrane thickness, and surface area.

Transport of Gases

Oxygen Transport: 97% transported as oxyhaemoglobin; 3% dissolved in plasma.

Carbon Dioxide Transport:

  • 70% as bicarbonates
  • 20‚Äì25% as carbamino-haemoglobin
  • 7% dissolved in plasma

Regulation of Respiration

The respiratory rhythm is controlled by the medulla (respiratory rhythm centre) and pons. Chemoreceptors respond to CO2 and H+ levels to adjust breathing rate.

Respiratory Disorders

  • Asthma: Inflammation and narrowing of bronchi.
  • Emphysema: Destruction of alveolar walls.
  • Bronchitis: Inflammation of bronchi.
  • Occupational lung diseases: Silicosis, asbestosis.

Summary

Breathing involves inhalation and exhalation, while gaseous exchange occurs in the alveoli. Oxygen and carbon dioxide are transported via blood using haemoglobin and bicarbonates. Respiration is regulated by neural and chemical systems, and various disorders affect respiratory health.
