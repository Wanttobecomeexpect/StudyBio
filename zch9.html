Chemical Coordination and Integration

Hormonal regulation, endocrine glands and how chemical signals integrate body functions together with nervous system.

Introduction to Endocrine System

Endocrine glands secrete hormones into blood to regulate physiology and development. Hormonal control is generally slower but longer lasting than neural control.

Gland Location Function
Pituitary Base of brain Master gland controlling other endocrine glands
Thyroid Neck Regulates metabolism

Hormones ‚Äî Types & Mode of Action

Hormones can be peptides/proteins, steroids, or amines. They act via receptors: membrane receptors (second messenger systems) or intracellular receptors (steroid hormones affecting gene expression).

Type Examples Mode of Action
Peptide Insulin, Glucagon Bind to membrane receptors, activate second messengers
Steroid Cortisol, Estrogen Bind to intracellular receptors, regulate gene expression

Major Endocrine Glands & Hormones

Key glands: hypothalamus/pituitary (master control), thyroid (metabolism), adrenal (stress response), pancreas (insulin/glucagon ‚Äî glucose homeostasis), gonads (sex hormones). Roles of major hormones are summarized.

Gland Hormone Function
Adrenal Adrenaline Fight or flight response
Pancreas Insulin Regulates blood glucose levels

Feedback Mechanisms

Hormone levels are regulated by negative (and sometimes positive) feedback loops ‚Äî maintain homeostasis (e.g., thyroid axis, blood glucose regulation by insulin/glucagon).

Feedback Type Example Outcome
Negative Thyroid hormone regulation Maintains stable hormone levels
Positive Oxytocin during childbirth Amplifies uterine contractions

Neural & Chemical Integration

Nervous and endocrine systems interact (neuroendocrine coordination) to integrate rapid and long-term responses (e.g., hypothalamo-pituitary axis). Both systems ensure organismal coordination across time scales.

System Component Role
Nervous Hypothalamus Controls pituitary gland
Endocrine Pituitary Secretes hormones to regulate other glands

Summary

Chemical coordination via hormones complements neural control. Understanding hormone types, glands, and feedback control is essential for physiology, medicine, and endocrinology.

Aspect Neural Control Hormonal Control
Speed Fast Slow
Duration Short-lived Long-lasting
